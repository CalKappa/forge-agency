Destination

The Serengeti

The Serengeti is, quite simply, the greatest wildlife arena on Earth. Its name — derived from the Maasai word "siringet" meaning "endless plains" — barely does justice to the 30,000 square kilometres of golden grassland, acacia woodland, and rocky kopjes that make up this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

This is the stage for the Great Migration, where over two million wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle make their annual circular journey in search of fresh grazing. It is a spectacle of raw, primal nature that has no equal anywhere in the world.

The Serengeti is also home to one of the densest lion populations in Africa, alongside leopards, cheetahs, elephants, giraffes, and over 500 species of birds. Every game drive here is different; every moment holds the possibility of something extraordinary.

Best time to visit: June–October for the dramatic river crossings of the northern migration. January–March for the calving season in the southern plains — a time of extraordinary predator action and newborn wildlife.

Ngorongoro Crater

Often described as "Africa's Garden of Eden," the Ngorongoro Crater is the world's largest intact volcanic caldera — a natural amphitheatre of breathtaking beauty and astonishing wildlife density. The crater floor, just 260 square kilometres, is home to approximately 25,000 large animals, including the elusive black rhinoceros.

Descending into the crater feels like entering another world. Flamingos line the soda lakes, elephants browse through lush acacia forests, and lions laze on the open grasslands. Here, you can see all of the Big Five in a single game drive — a feat few places on Earth can match.

The crater rim, rising 600 metres above the floor, is also home to the Maasai people, whose traditional bomas and cattle herds dot the highlands — a living reminder that this is a landscape shared between wildlife and culture.

Best time to visit: Year-round, though the dry season (June–October) offers the best wildlife viewing with less vegetation. The green season (November–May) brings migratory birds and lush, dramatic landscapes.

Destination

Tarangire National Park

Tarangire is Tanzania's best-kept secret — a park of ancient baobab trees, vast elephant herds, and a quieter, more intimate safari experience. During the dry season, the Tarangire River becomes a lifeline for the region's wildlife, drawing enormous concentrations of animals to its banks.

This is elephant country. Tarangire is home to some of the largest elephant herds in East Africa, and watching a family group move through the cathedral-like baobab woodland is one of the most moving experiences a safari can offer.

Beyond elephants, Tarangire is renowned for its tree-climbing pythons, resident leopards, large prides of lions, and over 550 species of birds — including many that are found nowhere else in the northern circuit.

Best time to visit: July–October, when the dry season concentrates wildlife along the Tarangire River. The green season (January–March) is excellent for birding and dramatic skies.

Destination

Lake Manyara

Tucked beneath the dramatic cliffs of the Great Rift Valley escarpment, Lake Manyara National Park is a compact gem of extraordinary diversity. Ernest Hemingway called it "the loveliest lake in Africa," and it's easy to see why.

The park is famous for its tree-climbing lions — a rare behaviour seen in only a few places in Africa — and for the vast flocks of flamingos that paint the alkaline lake shore pink during the wet season.

From dense groundwater forest to open grassland and the shimmering lake itself, Manyara packs an astonishing variety of habitats into a relatively small area. It's a perfect complement to the larger parks, offering a different pace and a chance to see species — like blue monkeys and giant kingfishers — that are harder to find elsewhere.

Best time to visit: June–October for tree-climbing lions and large mammals. November–May for flamingos and migratory birds.

Beach & Culture

Zanzibar

After the adventure of the bush, Zanzibar offers the perfect counterpoint — a place to decompress, to feel the warm Indian Ocean on your skin, and to explore a culture as rich and layered as the spices for which the island is famous.

The "Spice Island" is a world unto itself. In the UNESCO-listed Stone Town, narrow alleyways reveal a fascinating blend of African, Arab, Indian, and European influences. Beyond the town, pristine white-sand beaches stretch for miles, fringed by palm trees and lapped by waters of impossible turquoise.

Whether you want to snorkel over coral reefs, dive with dolphins, explore a spice plantation, sail on a traditional dhow at sunset, or simply lie in a hammock with a good book, Zanzibar delivers — and we handle every detail of the beach extension just as meticulously as the safari itself.

Good to Know

Planning Your Trip

Tanzania is a year-round destination, but the experience varies by season. June to October is the dry season — ideal for wildlife viewing as animals concentrate around water sources. This is also peak season for the Great Migration river crossings in the northern Serengeti. January to March is the calving season in the southern Serengeti, with excellent predator action and fewer crowds. November to May is the "green season" — lower prices, dramatic skies, lush landscapes, and superb birding. We'll help you choose the perfect time based on your priorities.

A typical safari day begins early — often before dawn — to catch the wildlife at its most active. You'll enjoy morning and afternoon game drives in a private 4×4 Land Cruiser with a pop-up roof, accompanied by your dedicated English or French-speaking guide. Midday is for resting at your lodge or camp, enjoying lunch, and perhaps taking a swim. Our guides are among the best in Tanzania — not just expert trackers, but warm, engaging storytellers who bring the bush to life.

We work with a curated selection of lodges and tented camps across every budget level — from comfortable mid-range properties to ultra-luxurious exclusive-use camps. Tented camps offer the most authentic safari experience: en-suite canvas tents with proper beds, hot showers, and often stunning views. Lodges provide a more traditional hotel-style experience with additional amenities. We know every property personally and will match your accommodation to your style, budget, and the experience you're looking for.

Safari costs vary significantly depending on the season, accommodation level, and duration. As a general guide: Mid-range safaris (comfortable lodges and camps) typically range from $300–$500 per person per day. Premium safaris (high-end lodges and luxury tented camps) range from $500–$1,000+. Ultra-luxury (exclusive-use, fly-in camps) can exceed $1,500 per person per day. These prices typically include accommodation, all meals, park fees, guiding, and game drives. International flights, travel insurance, and personal items are additional. We'll always work transparently within your budget.

Tanzania is a wonderful family destination. Children of all ages are welcome on private safaris, and we'll tailor the itinerary to suit your family — shorter drives, engaging activities, and family-friendly accommodation. Many lodges and camps offer dedicated children's programmes, bush walks, and cultural experiences designed for younger travellers. For very young children (under 5), we recommend lodges over tented camps and will advise on the best parks and properties. A family safari is one of the most enriching experiences you can share — children are mesmerised by the wildlife and the vastness of Africa.

Pack light and practical. Essential items include: neutral-coloured clothing (khaki, olive, beige — avoid bright colours and white), layers for cool mornings and warm afternoons, a good sun hat and sunglasses, SPF 50+ sunscreen, comfortable walking shoes, a warm fleece or jacket for evenings, binoculars (essential!), a camera with a zoom lens, and insect repellent. If you're flying between parks in light aircraft, soft-sided bags are required (typically 15–20kg maximum). We'll send you a detailed packing list tailored to your specific itinerary before departure.
