The Heart Behind Kibuyu

Born from a love of Tanzania, built on a promise of extraordinary care

OUR STORY

Your Safari, Our Passion

"Kibuyu" means baobab tree in Swahili — a symbol of strength, longevity, and deep roots in the African landscape. Just like the baobab, our company is rooted in a profound connection to this land and its people. Every safari we create is an expression of that connection.

Based in Arusha, the gateway to Tanzania's most iconic national parks, we work personally with every guest to understand their dreams, preferences, and sense of adventure. There are no templates here — only journeys as unique as the travellers who embark on them.

"We don't just plan safaris — We pour our heart into creating moments that will stay with you forever. Every sunrise over the Serengeti, every elephant encounter, every candlelit dinner under the stars is a memory we want you to treasure."

From the first conversation to the final farewell, we oversee every detail: the perfect lodges and camps for your style, the most experienced guides who know every hidden corner, the special touches — from a favourite wine waiting at camp to a surprise bush breakfast. This is personal, and it always will be.

WHY TANZANIA

The Ultimate Safari Destination

Tanzania is Africa at its most magnificent — a land of staggering diversity where the world's greatest wildlife spectacle unfolds across vast, untamed landscapes. From the endless plains of the Serengeti to the turquoise waters of Zanzibar, this is a country that captures the imagination like no other.

Home to some of the continent's most iconic national parks, Tanzania offers an unrivalled safari experience. The sheer concentration of wildlife, the dramatic variety of ecosystems, and the warmth of the Tanzanian people make this a destination that stays with you long after you return home.

  • The Great Migration — over two million wildebeest and zebra in an epic annual journey
  • The Big Five — lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino across multiple parks
  • Ngorongoro Crater — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and natural wonder
  • Mount Kilimanjaro — Africa's highest peak as a breathtaking backdrop
  • Zanzibar's pristine beaches — white sand, turquoise water, and rich Swahili culture
  • Maasai culture — authentic encounters with one of Africa's most iconic communities
